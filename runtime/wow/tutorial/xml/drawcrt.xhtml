Draw the Concept Relationships

The next most important step is to draw the various concept relationships.
For our example purposes, we will be basically using the "prerequisite" relationship.

  • The blank space which we see in the right of the concept heirarchy is called the "graph window". Concepts can be dragged to the "graph window" in order to include them in a concept relationship. Concepts in the "graph window" can be dragged to any location to make the graph more pleasing. To drag a concept inside the "graph window" you should not click exactly on the center of the rectangle representing the concept.

This screendump shows what the graph window looks like after we have dragged all the concepts of our example.

  • A relationship is created of the current type, so the type should be selected first, before you draw the relationship. Select the "prerequisite" type as shown here.
  • To draw concept relationships you must start by clicking in the center of the rectangle and then dragging an arrow to the destination of the relationship.
    Drawing an arrow from A to B means that "A is a prerequisite for B". This means that it is advisable to read A before B.
    In our example we have following prerequisites
    • "welcome" is a prerequisite for "history", "basic", "advanced" and "final"
    • "basic" is a prerequisite for "advanced", "final", "advanced" and all its children ("document", "lists", "textstructure").
    • "advanced" is a prerequisite for "final", and all its children ("special", "anchors").

This is what our graph window looks like after we have drawn all the relationships.

Note : The structure of composition of concepts must be a hierarchy, or a directed acyclic graph. No concept can thus be a descendent of itself either directly or indirectly.
